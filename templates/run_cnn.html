Understanding Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) is a class of deep learning algorithms designed to process structured grid data, such as images, audio, or text. In this project, CNNs are used to detect fake news by analyzing both the textual content and images of news articles in real-time.

CNNs excel in image classification and feature extraction by applying convolutional layers to detect patterns and structures within data. For fake news detection, CNNs analyze the news articleâ€™s text, detect contextual patterns, and identify whether the content is likely to be genuine or manipulated.

  • Feature Extraction: CNNs automatically extract relevant features from raw data without manual intervention, making them highly efficient for detecting subtle patterns in text and images.
  • Real-time Processing: CNNs process large volumes of data in real-time, making them ideal for detecting fake news on platforms like social media or news websites.
  • Textual Analysis: In this project, CNNs use word embeddings to analyze the semantic structure of text and identify misleading patterns, ensuring the accuracy of the detection system.
  • Multimodal Capabilities: Besides text, CNNs can also detect fake images or videos associated with news content, leveraging their ability to identify alterations in multimedia formats.
